2010 Presidential Tracking Polls (1ยบ Turno, First Round)

The field was far thinner than in 1989 or today, which shows that the PSDB-PT alternation hegemony was still in tact. When he left office, Lula's popularity ratings were the highest of any world leader ever recorded at 87 percent (with an also-astronomical 80 percent of respondents approving of his government).




Serra and Dilma's tracking polls diverged at a late-June inflection point, right after the party nominating conventions. The PT convention, held on June 13, 2010, which held a pro forma vote to officialize Dilma as their candidate (and Temer as her vice-president, a nod to how effectively the PMDB had quietly consolidated political power in the preceding decate). At the nominating convention, Dilma said that she would "continue Lula's Brazil" but "with the heart and soul of a woman." This was when the campaigns ramped up, and Lula appeared on TV and at rallies nearly every day on behalf of Dilma). The race never really got close after that; Dilma beat Serra by 12 million votes (12.1 percentage points) in the runoff.
